Cybercrime Offense Categories

Categorization of critical digital threats and criminal activities.

Cyber-Enabled Offenses

These are offenses committed with or without technology but increased their reach using such technologies. [Image of phishing email and login form]

  • Identity Theft & Fraud:
  • • Fraudulent impersonation to gain advantage or obtain property.
  • Phishing:
  • • Luring users to fake websites to gather sensitive information (password, PIN, account number).

Cyber-Dependent Offenses: Hacking

Offenses strictly committed using ICT, directly targeting network systems, data confidentiality, integrity, and availability. [Image of network intrusion detection system console]

  • • Unauthorized access (logical or physical entry without credentials).
  • • Modification/Impairment of data.
  • • Interception of data (unauthorized alteration in transmission).
  • • Misuse of assets.

Cyber-Dependent Offenses: Malware

Software-based hacking tools intentionally malicious, targeting systems for destruction or data theft.

  • • Virus (self-replicates by changing programs).
  • • Worm (standalone, replicates without human intervention).
  • • Trojan (appears legitimate, but contains malicious payload).
  • • Spyware (captures banking details, screenshots, etc.).

National Security Offenses

Cyberterrorism: unlawful offenses engaging in terrorist activities against critical infrastructure. [Image of cyber attack on power grid]

  • • Incursion (sudden invasive attacks on infrastructure).
  • • Destruction (destroying data to make it unreadable/inaccessible).
  • • DDoS (Disrupting networks by making them inaccessible).
  • • Defacement of Websites.

Cybersecurity Defense Technologies

Key defense mechanisms for protecting systems and data integrity.

01

Encryption (Cryptography)

The technology of coding plaintext into unreadable ciphertext using encryption keys and algorithms. It protects against illegal and unauthorized access. [Image of asymmetric encryption process]

**Examples:** RSA, TDEA, Blowfish, Twofish.

Plaintext + Key = Ciphertext
Ensures privacy and confidentiality during transmission and storage.
02

Biometrics and Authentication

**Authentication** validates the identity of an end user. **Biometrics** uses unique physical/behavioral traits (eyes, fingerprints, face) for higher security. [Image of biometric scanner]

**Examples:** Optical/Ultrasonic/Capacitive Scanners, Access Tokens, Security Keys.

Validation: User ID + Security Key/Trait
Traits (e.g., fingerprint) are harder to lose and duplicate than traditional passwords.
03

Firewall

The first line of defense, serving as a barrier between networks. It blocks specific inbound packet types and eliminates unauthorized data access based on defined IT policies. [Image of network firewall blocking traffic]

**Examples:** Network Address Translation (NAT), Packet-Filtering, VPNs.

> Inbound packet check: Policy defined...
> Protocol: HTTP (Port 80) -> BLOCKED
> Protocol: HTTPS (Port 443) -> ALLOWED
04

Virus Detection (Anti-Malware)

Software that prevents, detects, and removes malicious software. It scans new programs or files for matching virus signatures, flagging infected programs for blacklisting or isolation.

**Examples:** On-Demand Scans, On-Access Scans, Boot-Time Scans.

> Scanning new file: 'update.exe'...
> Signature Match: Win32/TrojanDownloader
> Action: ISOLATE & BLACKLIST
05

Phishing Detection

Technologies used to identify and prevent evolving phishing attacks by checking visual similarity, blacklists, and performing web crawling to find faults in web indexes. [Image of phishing detection flow chart]

**Types:** Visual Similarity-Based, Blacklist-Based, Web Crawling-Based (WC-PAD).

> Checking URL against DNS Blacklist...
> Visual Similarity Score: 98% (High Risk)
> Action: SEND WARNING TO USER
